Internet On Your Palm

(Not Quite) - Source: MIT Technology Review

According to Technology Review's article, Afghani individuals defined as kars now carry portions of the internet in physical drives to share them with Afghanis without internet connection. Having been derived of their freedom and manifestly their internet access, Afghanis now rely on kars to sell them segments of the information superhighway. Movies, "music", and a few other types of content are transferred from neighbouring countries, stored in physical drives and sold off. They continue to sell these tokens of sanity while openly expressing their fear of execution.

Accounts like these are a constant reminder of our overlooked privilege.
